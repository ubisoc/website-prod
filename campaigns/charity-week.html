CHARITY WEEK IS APPROACHING

Every year UBISOC and other Isoc’s across the country participate in Charity Week to raise awareness and money for a charity cause. It is a week of fun and commitment, that relies largely on our volunteers! It’s taking place from the 6th to the 12th of November! Make sure you get stuck in!!

This year we are fundraising for an emergency appeal (& medical aid) in Yemen. An estimated 18.8 million people in Yemen need humanitarian or protection assistance. The cholera epidemic has now reached nearly 740,000 cases with 2,118 reported deaths. From the 6th till the 12th November UBISOC needs your help to make a difference. Charity Week in general

The University of Birmingham Islamic Society runs Charity Week during Week 7 of Term (Start of November). We choose a Charity and cause each year to fundraise for and then go all out in our efforts to raise more than the previous year. We organise a Charity Dinner, a sponsored climb, sell the iconic Krispy Kreme Doughnuts and much much more!

Gallery

From Charity Quiz to Charity Dinner, from sponsored climb in Snowdon to bucket collections, Charity Week 2016 has been a great week in support of a great cause.

University of Birmingham Islamic Society do it a little differently to other Islamic Societies. We recruit volunteers to be part of sub teams for Charity Week which organise specific parts of Charity Week like the a Charity Quiz. Everyone is welcome to join a team and we have Planning Meetings a few weeks before Charity Week where you can attend and join a team and plan Charity Week events with them. It's a great opportunity to volunteer for a fantastic cause and build up your own organisational and team working skills.

If you would like to get involved, please email: Anas Abu (vicebrother@ubisoc.co.uk).
This year, we are raising money for Human Appeal International (https://donate.humanappeal.org.uk).

Previous Charity Weeks by the Islamic Society

Previous Charity Weeks have been a huge success. Last year we raised over £26,000 for a Water Sanitation Project in Bangladesh. The year before we raised around £25,000 for the Zaatari Refugee Camp in Jordan.
